What We Do

Robotics Fundamentals

We explore the intersection of science, engineering, and technology that produces machines called robots. Our members learn mechanical design, electrical systems, programming, and problem-solving.

Competition Ready

We participate in the FIRST LEGO League challenges, building and programming autonomous robots to complete missions. Our teams compete at local, regional, and national levels.

Team Collaboration

Our club fosters teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills. Students work together in specialized roles as builders, coders, and managers to achieve common goals.

Why Join Robotics Club?

Our club gives students hands-on experience with real robotics systems. You'll learn how to design and build functional robots, programming logic and computational thinking, engineering principles through LEGO robotics, and teamwork and competition strategies.

At year's end, top teams compete in inter-school tournaments with the chance to advance to Nationals!

SPIKE Prime & FLL Challenges

Our tools for robotic innovation

LEGO Education SPIKE Prime System

The SPIKE Prime system is LEGO Education's flagship robotics platform that we use in our club. It combines colorful LEGO building elements, a programmable hub, motors and sensors, and a drag-and-drop coding interface based on Scratch with Python options.

Why We Use SPIKE Prime:

  • Perfect balance of simplicity for beginners and complexity for advanced users
  • Seamless transition from block coding to text-based Python programming
  • Robust hardware designed specifically for classroom and competition use
  • Direct compatibility with FIRST LEGO League challenge mats and missions

FIRST LEGO League Challenges

Each year, FIRST LEGO League (FLL) releases a new themed challenge with two components

Robot Game

Teams build and program autonomous robots to complete missions on a 4'x8' field mat:

  • 3-5 missions must be completed in 2.5 minutes
  • Scoring based on precision, efficiency, and innovation
  • 2025-2026 theme: UNEARTHED (archaeology focus)

Innovation Project

Teams research and develop solutions to real-world problems related to the season's theme:

  • Identify a specific problem within the theme
  • Design an innovative solution
  • Present findings to judges (5 minute presentation)
  • 2025-2026 focus: Solving challenges in archaeological discovery

FLL Competition Structure

Teams are judged in four key areas during tournaments

1

Robot Design

Evaluation of mechanical construction, programming sophistication, and strategy

2

Robot Performance

Scoring based on completed missions during competition matches

3

Innovation Project

Quality of research, creativity of solution, and presentation skills

4

Core Values

Demonstration of teamwork, gracious professionalism, and learning spirit

Tournament progression: Local → Regional → National → World Festival

Meet Our Team

Our dedicated members and their roles

Builders

The Builders are the hands-on engineers of our team. They design and construct the physical robot structure, select and assemble mechanical components, test and optimize robot movement and functionality, solve physical engineering challenges, and work with LEGO Technic and other building systems.

Coders

The Coders bring our robots to life through programming. They write and debug robot control software, program autonomous routines and sensor integration, develop efficient algorithms for competition tasks, work with languages like Python, Java, or block coding, and collaborate with builders to match code to hardware.

Managers

The Managers keep our team organized and focused. They coordinate team schedules and meetings, manage competition logistics and documentation, facilitate communication between team members, track progress and help solve team challenges, and organize resources and maintain equipment.
