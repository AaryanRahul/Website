What is Robotics?

Robotics is the intersection of science, engineering, and technology that produces machines called robots. At Henderson High School Robotics Club, we explore how robots can be programmed to perform tasks and solve problems through:

  • Mechanical design - Building robot structures
  • Electrical systems - Powering and controlling robots
  • Computer programming - Giving robots intelligence
  • Problem solving - Completing complex challenges

LEGO Education SPIKE Prime System

The SPIKE Prime system is LEGO Education's flagship robotics platform that we use in our club. It combines:

  • Colorful LEGO building elements - For durable, creative constructions
  • Programmable Hub - The brain of the robot with 6 input/output ports
  • Motors and sensors - Including force, color, distance and gyro sensors
  • Drag-and-drop coding interface - Based on Scratch with Python options

Why We Use SPIKE Prime:

  • Perfect balance of simplicity for beginners and complexity for advanced users
  • Seamless transition from block coding to text-based Python programming
  • Robust hardware designed specifically for classroom and competition use
  • Direct compatibility with FIRST LEGO League challenge mats and missions

FIRST LEGO League Challenges

Each year, FIRST LEGO League (FLL) releases a new themed challenge with two components:

ü§ñ Robot Game ü§ñ

Teams build and program autonomous robots to complete missions on a 4'x8' field mat:

  • 3-5 missions must be completed in 2.5 minutes
  • Scoring based on precision, efficiency, and innovation
  • 2025-2026 theme: UNEARTHED (archaeology focus)

üí° Innovation Project üí°

Teams research and develop solutions to real-world problems related to the season's theme:

  • Identify a specific problem within the theme
  • Design an innovative solution
  • Present findings to judges (5 minute presentation)
  • 2025-2026 focus: Solving challenges in archaeological discovery

FLL Competition Structure

Teams are judged in four key areas during tournaments:

1. Robot Design

Evaluation of mechanical construction, programming sophistication, and strategy

2. Robot Performance

Scoring based on completed missions during competition matches

3. Innovation Project

Quality of research, creativity of solution, and presentation skills

4. Core Values

Demonstration of teamwork, gracious professionalism, and learning spirit

Our Robotics Club Roles

üõ† BUILDER üõ†

The Builders are the hands-on engineers of our team. They:

  • Design and construct the physical robot structure
  • Select and assemble mechanical components
  • Test and optimize robot movement and functionality
  • Solve physical engineering challenges
  • Work with LEGO Technic and other building systems

üíª CODER üíª

The Coders bring our robots to life through programming. They:

  • Write and debug robot control software
  • Program autonomous routines and sensor integration
  • Develop efficient algorithms for competition tasks
  • Work with languages like Python, Java, or block coding
  • Collaborate with builders to match code to hardware

üëë MANAGER üëë

The Managers keep our team organized and focused. They:

  • Coordinate team schedules and meetings
  • Manage competition logistics and documentation
  • Facilitate communication between team members
  • Track progress and help solve team challenges
  • Organize resources and maintain equipment

Every member contributes to our success, and students often rotate roles to gain experience in all areas of robotics!
